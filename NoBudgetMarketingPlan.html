No Marketing Budge​t ? how to run a game marketing campaign DIY

May 25, 2024 / By VISHARAD UPADHYAY

Marketing is frequently perceived as a costly endeavor beyond the reach of small developers, but it doesn’t have to be. Even without a marketing budget, there are numerous strategies to attract attention to your game! Even if you intend to handle everything independently, you must consider the significant time investment required to execute a successful marketing campaign. However, for small game studios, time is often their only resource. If you’re searching for effective, cost-free methods to generate interest in your game, you’ve landed in the right spot!

1 / Create a press kit

So the next time some press wants to cover your game but don’t, because they can't find enough content and pre-made text about your game. Ex. Screenshots, videos, gifs and a whole pitch and beautifully written description of your game and team.
A fresh press kit equips journalists and creators with the perfect assets – logo, header, and curated screenshots – for a flawless first impression.

Key Art | 16:9
Box Art | 9:16 or 4:3
Thumbnail | 1:1
Game & company logo | without background
Trailer | .mp4 or YouTube embed (preferably both)
Best possible screenshots & GIFs

2 / Create a website

With your game launching across platforms, a website acts as your central hub. Gamers searching online (not just within app stores) will find your website first, thanks to the power of search engines.  Make sure to include:

Store links
Most recent trailer front & center
Link to Press Kit
Link to contact form or email
List your official social media channels
A form to sign up for your newsletter
A call to action for content creators (“Fill out this form to request a review key”)

3/ Optimise your store pages for maximum conversion

Take all of the steps listed above and some of the ones you’ll find in more detail in the steps below and apply these to your own store pages, we’ll use Steam as an example here but the same applies for Epic Games, PlayStation, Xbox, the Switch eShop and others…

Pick a title that is unique and can’t be confused with other games
Have a killer key art that shows what your game is about
Have a gameplay trailer that gets right into the action
Choose your best screenshots
Describe your game’s genre, style and unique hooks in the short description
Use an engaging description separated with visual headers or animated GIFs
Use bullet points to make it easier to read (avoid walls of text)
Build your wishlist. As soon as you have a page, make it your main Call To Action.
Try to get to 10 reviews ASAP

4 / Sign up for an IndieDB page

IndieDB is a site where you can find pretty much all the indies around,  IndieDB is a one-stop shop for discovering indie games, but maintaining your profile requires constant updates. The good news? Their strong Google ranking means your uploaded media has a high chance of appearing in image searches. Just remember, outdated info might reflect poorly if journalists use those assets!

Visual by Matt Hackett

6 / Invest in great artwork

Perhaps one of the most important tools that will get people to click on your game in a list, is having the best possible art. 

Readable Title (high contrast)
Art that stops the player from scrolling
Recognizable?
Conveys the genre of the game

7 / Make a trailer

Landing a click on your thumbnail is just the first hurdle. Once players arrive on your page, the trailer becomes the key that unlocks their engagement. It's your sole chance to make a stellar first impression, so pour your heart (and editing skills) into crafting a trailer that truly shines.

Start with the best moments, catch them in the first 5 seconds
Avoid black frames at the start or a slow fade in
Keep it short (1m30sec max)
don’t tell (avoid on-screen text where you can)
Skip the company & engine logos (or leave them for the end)
End with a CTA: Wishlist or buy

8 / The Goat, SOCIAL MEDIA

Social Media is the best way to build a community and reach that audience that is actually interested in your game, thanks to the fact that it's all organic traffic.
But your social media isn't just a megaphone for your game. Use it to connect! Engage with press, gamers, and fellow devs.  Just remember to sprinkle in your own game content so visitors know what you're about. Pin your most important post for maximum impact!

9 / and the KINGG, DEMO

Consider launching a demo to supercharge your campaign. This one-two punch gets your game in the "FREE" category for high visibility, lets players try before they buy, and might even spark content creation. Plus, a demo itself becomes valuable marketing material if other parts of the game are still in the works. It can get you email registration for your email to what not.

10 / Build a community

prioritize a Discord server! While social media forms a loose community, Discord offers a dedicated space for open chat, feedback sessions, and rallying support for your latest initiative. It's your hub for deeper engagement with your audience.

Need feedback on the game? Ask your community
Need 10 Steam reviews? Ask your community
Need hype in a Twitch chat? Ask your community
Have an event or activation you want to promote?

11 / Press Release

Nailing a press release can feel like a boss battle, but fret not! Here's a breakdown to get you started, Seeking Help? For polished releases, consider a PR agency. You might be surprised by affordable options (we offer them too, wink wink).

DIY Release? No problem! Head over to Games Press for stellar examples to mimic their winning structure. Think catchy title, short intro, and developer/game info at the end.
Stand Out From the Crowd: Imagine the journalist bombarded with releases. Hook them fast! What's newsworthy about your game? Highlight that first, then layer on secondary details to keep them engaged.

12 / Best Friend : Review Keys

New to the scene? Your press release might not be a silver bullet. But offering review keys to journalists and content creators can be a game-changer.
Be Realistic: Don't expect giants like IGN on day one (unless your game's a viral phenomenon).
Target Niche Powerhouses: Reviews on a relevant niche site (like a 100k+ monthly view VR site for your VR game) can be more impactful than a general giant.
The Snowball Effect: Coverage from smaller outlets and creators can spark word-of-mouth, eventually grabbing the attention of the big players.
Distributing Your Keys:
Your List: Start with your existing community.
Key Distribution Services: Consider using services like Keymailer, Lurkit, PressEngine, or IndieGameCollective (many options exist!).
Beware the Flood: Vetting requests takes time! You'll encounter fakes and imposters. Allocate time for this pre-launch and follow up afterwards.
